Activism, Citizenry and Ethics in Software


Reflecting on Martin Luther King Jr.'s stance for justice and ethics, my journey in software development echoes a similar need for principled stands. My career, transitioning from technical roles to software development, hit a moral crossroad at a pharmaceutical company. There, I developed a health survey that--unbeknownst to me, was misleading, targeting specific demographics. This ethical lapse was clear when a tragedy linked to the health survey and drug I worked on came to light. Resigning in the wake of this realization, I understood the gravity of ethical integrity in our work, much like Dr. King understood in his fight for justice. This incident was a stark reminder of our duty as developers to uphold ethics, especially in an era where technology like AI and ChatGPT holds immense influence.



As software engineers, we must be vigilant and act when our morals or ethics are in conflict with the work we are asked to produce and the products we provide. We must strive to make and leave our communities better than we inherited them, and that starts with being aware of the immediate and long-term impact of the software we build.
